First days

The first time I entered the Significa's office I was amazed. Coolest workspace ever!

A big surprise for me was how young everyone was, as well as the number of people that worked here.

A mix of on-site and remote work was kind of a novelty for me. What at first already felt like a lot of people then grew in perspective.

My first days combined a mix of excitement and fear, feeling a little bit overwhelmed by how new everything was.

I had no idea about all the different roles that can take part on a digital agency. I even believed that Nuno was a designer.

The internal pressure to do good and to not mess up was very real.

Despite of this the atmosphere was very easy going. I felt really welcomed as everyone was so kind and friendly.

On my first days I worked on the design challenges and that was definitely A challenge...

My belief that I knew very little of the UI sphere was amplified. Gladly the amazing design team was there to help me do better :)

Still those challenges should remain in the trash until further notice.

/10

Learnings

Many were the learnings that I took from this experience, but some were quite remarkable. Here they go:

Don't be afraid to make mistakes. Explore, explore and explore.

You can only be as good as your references. Choose them wisely.

Team work makes the dream work.

The client isn't always right. We are the experts.

Good communication is the key.

Always ask the three questions. What, how and why.

First learn the rationale, then explore the tools. The tools change constantly, the basis does not.

/8

Experience

My experience at Significa over these last 4 months definitely surpassed my expectations.

I never thought that I would be able to work on real projects as an intern. So the U.Dream and the Peladrone projects were an exciting surprise.

Working on both of these allowed me to gain a lot of insight on what goes into the making of an interface other than the design itself.

The facilitation that occurs between the designer, the PM and the client are essential for a projects success.

Not to forget as well, the collaboration with the fellow designers and developers.

Every project has its own needs and wants but it's the lines of communication that are transversal to any.

When it comes to the design, these projects allowed me to get in touch with the methodology of UI design.

At the same time I was able to explore two different realms: the website and the app.

Something very important and valuable for my learning was being able to get a closer look on everyone's projects and way of working.

In retrospective, something that I didn't do but feel like is very relevant would be to understand the developers concerns and needs more closely, specially the frontend.

Looking back I can see a huge evolution. This experience was very impactful and I can't thank you enough for your generosity, patience and support.

A big thank you to you all! <3
